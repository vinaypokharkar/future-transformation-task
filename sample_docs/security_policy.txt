Acme Corporation — Information Security Policy

Scope

This policy applies to every employee, contractor, and third party with access
to Acme systems or data.

Passwords and Authentication

Passwords must be at least twelve characters long and must not be reused across
services. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all administrative
accounts and for any system exposed to the public internet. Shared accounts are
prohibited; where a shared credential is unavoidable, it must be stored in the
company password manager and rotated whenever a team member leaves.

Device Security

Laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled before any company data is
copied to them. Screens must lock automatically after ten minutes of
inactivity. Lost or stolen devices must be reported to the security team
immediately, and in any case within four hours of discovery.

Data Classification

Data is classified as Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted.
Confidential and Restricted data must never be copied to personal devices or
personal cloud storage accounts. Restricted data additionally requires
encryption at rest and access logging.

Incident Reporting

Suspected security incidents must be reported to the security team without
delay. Staff must not attempt to investigate or remediate an incident
themselves, as this can destroy evidence needed for analysis.

Third-Party Access

Vendors requiring system access must sign a data processing agreement before
access is granted. Access is time-limited and reviewed quarterly.
